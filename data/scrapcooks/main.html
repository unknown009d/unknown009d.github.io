Scrapcooks

Create, savor, waste less.

An AI-powered solution that transforms last night's leftovers into creative, unique recipes, helping reduce food waste while offering delicious new meal ideas.

June 2025 - July 2025

Tired of finding recipes that always require ingredients you don't have?

Transform your leftovers into delicious, unique meals with AI-powered suggestions designed around what's already in your kitchen.

User Research

Found out that everyday cooking is often hindered by time constraints, mismatched ingredients, and recipe platforms demanding unavailable items. This leads to decision fatigue, causing many to skip meals or rely on food delivery instead.

The insights highlight a strong need for a solution that generates simple recipes using available ingredients, reducing effort, minimizing food waste, and making home cooking more accessible and stress-free.


Storyboards

User pain points

Meal Decision

Users often feel mentally exhausted trying to decide what to cook with limited ingredients and little time.

Unhelpful Recipe

Most online recipes require extra ingredients or don't align with the random leftovers users have.

Time Constraints

Busy students and professionals don't have time to browse long recipe lists or shop for missing ingredients.

Food waste

Leftovers frequently go unused because users don't know how to combine them into a proper meal.

Usability Study

Conducted an online unmoderate usability study for around 10 minutes and asked for feedback on overall ease of use, confidence completing task, navigating the app.

Findings

After usability study we found out people were confused after searching because of back button as well as no label on the icons made it more confused which lead to 40% drop-off rate :

Refining Design

After the usability test there were few design changes that I took are discussed below:

Problem #1

After having the pain points from the usability test I prioritized and resolved the critical usability issues identified, that were back button and a consistent top bar, this is a huge concern for accessibility which was fixed by adding a back button in all pages and a consistent top bar.

Problem #2

Users also had issues with navigation thus, created a navigation bar with label, this also ensures that accessibility concerns. It is much more prominent and users can identify each icons distinctly.

Choosing Colors

The color palette evokes warmth, creativity, and comfort.

The soft beige suggests home-cooked simplicity, the deep brown hints at rich, hearty flavors, and the vibrant coral adds a fresh, appetizing energy perfect for turning leftovers into delightful meals.

Thus the last color is used in cooking instruction to convey a sense of achievement for the user.

Conclusion

The main objective was to create an platform where people can make their customized recipe with the leftover food that they have. It reduces food waste and create unique food items.

This project taught me that as much as I want to make the user-flow simple it requires a lot of iteration to make it perfect for normal usage. Not giving an back button was a design choice but after usability test most of the users wanted to have a back button because they think they have done something wrong. And the back button is a sign of relief (just like undo)
